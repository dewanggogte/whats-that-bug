## How to Research New Topics

1. Search Google for the target keyword. Note what's ranking in the top 10.
2. Check "People also ask" and "Related searches" at the bottom of the page.
3. If the top results are thin (listicles, pest control ads, outdated content), the keyword is worth pursuing.
4. If the top results are from high-authority sites with deep content (Wikipedia, Smithsonian, university extension sites), consider a more specific angle.
5. Use Google Trends to check if the keyword has seasonal patterns.
